## Stage 1 Coaching Notes

**Concept type:** Commercial product — health module/layer extending existing LifePilot platform.

**Customer confirmed:** Ambitious busy professional (30–45, married/partnered, career-driven). Primary user is the builder — the persona is personal, not constructed.

**Core problem statement:** Health consistently deprioritized despite genuine care; lack of body self-awareness; no existing system connects life context (stress, relationships, purpose, culture) to health guidance. Apps address the 20% (physical metrics) and ignore the 80% (social determinants).

**Differentiator:** Not a feature advantage — a structural data moat. LifePilot already holds mood history, goal patterns, stress signals, and purpose alignment that no standalone health app can access.

**Key competitive context:**
- Google Health Coach launched May 19, 2026 (4 days ago) — validates category from metrics/wearable angle
- SDOH consumer market is wide open — no product has successfully productized social health for professionals
- App fatigue is real: 45% burnout rate, avg user juggles 6 health apps
- 70–80% churn within 90 days is the sector's documented failure — retention is the hard problem

**Landmine identified:** "Social health" and "social determinants" carry clinical/low-income associations — avoid this framing in consumer copy. Use: "your life context," "what's actually going on in your life," "the whole picture."

**North-star sentence (from brainstorming):** "My best body who knows me so well, and always gives me the right guidance."

**Assumptions challenged during ignition:**
- "Too many apps in the market" → answered by structural differentiation, not feature differentiation
- Vision is the 2030 Zero-Friction Stack; MVP is Body Check + Social Health briefing logic — press release must be honest about sequencing

**Initial concepts explored but not used:**
- "Digital health twin" framing — too technical
- SDOH clinical language — too clinical/niche for target demographic
- "Wellness" positioning — too spa/yoga, wrong for ambitious professional demographic
