## Technique Execution Results

### Phase 1 — Inner Child Conference

**Core Breakthrough:** LifePilot currently measures *proxies* of health. The inner child measures health itself — am I suffering? Am I comfortable? Am I happy right now? The gap between those two things is where the most interesting product territory lives.

**Key Exchanges:**
- *"Why does it only ask once a day?"* → overwhelm → the app asks, it doesn't sense. Led to passive intelligence ideas.
- *"Can't it just tell?"* → the expectation that a caring thing already knows → Silent Watcher concept.
- *"Why does it send another one tomorrow? Does it forget?"* → tomorrow is a new day, new feeling → Amnesia Mode, Daily Renewal theme.
- *"Talking to a machine is weird"* → but not because of talking — because it pretends to be a friend → Honest Tool, Familiar Stranger.
- *"How do I know it's working?"* → working = happy, no pain, not hungry, thirsty → the WHO definition of health from a child's mouth → Body Check, Needs Layer, Suffering Signal.

### Phase 2 — Future Self Interview

**North-Star Sentence (2030):** *"My best body who knows me so well, and always gives me the right guidance."*

**Key Exchanges:**
- *"Who did LifePilot help most?"* → It's me — married, ambitious, busy, struggling to prioritise health, seeking self-awareness. The builder IS the primary user.
- *"What does a Tuesday look like?"* → Meal recommended or restaurant chosen → shopping list → order placed → gym scheduled → routine ready. Guided without effort.
- *"What almost stopped it?"* → Too many apps already on the market with the same intent.
- *"What did they all miss?"* → They looked at questionnaire patterns. LifePilot looked at the broader social profile of the person.

## Action Plans

### ⚡ #6 Body Check — Ships Next Sprint

1. Extend `CheckinSchema` with `bodyState`: pain (none/mild/significant), energy (low/medium/high), comfort (comfortable/tense/unwell)
2. Add step to check-in page phase state machine — after mood, before submission
3. Store in `checkins` table; pass to briefing generation prompt
4. Add to weekly summary aggregation

**Success signal:** Briefing says *"Your body has been tense three mornings this week — worth noticing."*

### 🏆 #20 Social Health Model — Framework Sprint

1. Define **Social Health Index** — private score from existing data: goal completion rate, mood trend, check-in consistency, briefing engagement
2. Write design doc: how briefing health recommendations shift when social health is low vs. high
3. Add two optional monthly check-in questions: *"How connected do you feel to the people around you?"* and *"How meaningful does your work feel right now?"*
4. This framework becomes the PRFAQ brief for LifePilot Health

**Success signal:** Briefing health guidance visibly differs for a high-stress week vs. a stable week.

### 🎯 #13 Zero-Friction Stack — North Star Planning

1. Write capability map: integrations needed (calendar, restaurant/grocery, fitness, recipe APIs)
2. Identify first wedge: meal suggestion in briefing based on body check energy + food preferences — no ordering yet
3. Define preference data model: food culture, dietary constraints, gym access, workout style — added to onboarding profile
4. Evaluate integration partners: Deliveroo/Uber Eats, Google Calendar, Strava/Apple Health

**Success signal:** Morning briefing includes *"Based on your low energy and protein goal, here's a 15-minute meal"* with shopping list.

## Session Summary

**24 ideas generated** across 2 techniques, organised into 5 themes, with 3 prioritised for action.

**The core thesis that emerged:**
> LifePilot's health direction is not "another health app." It is the first health system that knows the whole person — their life, their social context, their goals, their stresses — and uses that knowledge to make health effortless, personalised, and meaningful. The differentiator is not a feature. It is a data moat that no standalone health app can replicate.

**Key creative breakthroughs:**
- A 7-year-old defined health better than most health apps: no pain, no hunger, happy. The WHO definition, unprompted.
- The primary user is the builder — this is personal. That's a strength, not a limitation.
- The answer to market saturation was found in 2030: they looked at questionnaire patterns. We look at the social profile.
- "My best body who knows me so well" — not a product description, a relationship description.

**What makes LifePilot uniquely positioned:**
LifePilot already has the data that matters most for health — mood history, goal patterns, life events, stress signals, purpose alignment. The health features don't need to be built from scratch. They need to be surfaced from what's already there.
